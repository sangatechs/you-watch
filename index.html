A personal watchlist is the difference between an app you use occasionally and one you return to every match day. This two-level system means your watchlist remains useful whether you're following a specific team through a season or just want the best channel for any given sport each night.

HOW TO USE IT

Five Ways to Make Favourites Work for You

⭐

Star Your Most-Watched Channels

Long-press any channel in the channel list to add it to favourites. Starred channels appear at the top of the channel list for instant access without scrolling through the full catalogue.

🔔

Enable Match Notifications

Turn on notifications for your favourite teams or leagues to receive an alert when a match is about to begin. Never arrive at the app twenty minutes into a match because you forgot it was on.

📋

Create Sport-Specific Lists

Organise your watchlist by sport rather than keeping everything in one list. Separate cricket and football favourites mean you navigate directly to the right sport without scrolling past irrelevant channels.

🔄

Update Your List Seasonally

Tournament seasons end and new ones begin. Review your watchlist at the start of each major season to add new competitions and remove those that have concluded, keeping the list current and useful.

📱

Sync Across Devices

If you watch on both a phone and a TV device, ensure your watchlist settings are preserved across devices by using the same account configuration on both. Consistent access regardless of which device you pick up.

Practical tip: Limit your active favourites list to twelve to fifteen channels. A longer list becomes as difficult to navigate as no list at all. Curate for quality — the channels you actually watch most — rather than adding everything that looks potentially interesting.
